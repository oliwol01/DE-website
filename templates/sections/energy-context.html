Nuclear energy remains part of the EU’s long-term decarbonisation strategy.

According to the European Commission, achieving the EU’s climate and energy targets will require multiple low-carbon energy solutions, including both renewable and nuclear electricity production. While some member states are reducing or phasing out nuclear power, others continue to expand it as part of their long-term decarbonisation, energy security, and industrial competitiveness strategies. These differences contribute to significant variation in carbon emissions across Europe.
